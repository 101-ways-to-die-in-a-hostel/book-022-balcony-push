# Location Profile

## The Hostel

**Name:** Casa Barcelo Rooftop Hostel
**Type:** Party Hostel / Boutique Hybrid
**Star Rating:** 3.5
**Price Range:** EUR 28-50 per night

### History
Built in 1890 as a textile merchant's residence. Converted to apartments in the 1960s, fell into disrepair by the 1990s. Bought and renovated in 2015 by a Dutch entrepreneur who saw potential in the rooftop terrace. The building retains original Catalan modernist features - decorative ironwork, tile floors, ornate balconies - but the rooftop is all modern bar and lounge.

### Architecture
- **Style:** Catalan Modernist with modern rooftop addition
- **Floors:** 5 (4 accommodation + rooftop bar)
- **Total beds:** 78
- **Notable features:** Original wrought-iron balconies on each floor, rooftop bar with panoramic city views, preserved mosaic tiles in common areas

### Layout
```
Ground Floor:
- Reception
- Luggage storage
- Small courtyard (original building feature)
- Street access to narrow Gothic Quarter lane

Floors 1-3:
- Mixed dormitories (6, 8, 10-bed)

Floor 4:
- Private rooms

Rooftop:
- Bar and lounge area

- Corner balcony overlooking Carrer dels Comtes (the death location)
```

### The Death Location (Specific)
**Where exactly does the death occur?**
The corner balcony on the rooftop level. It's a small extension of the terrace that juts out over Carrer dels Comtes, a narrow lane four stories below. The railing is original wrought iron from 1890 - beautiful, decorative, but only 90cm high (modern code requires 110cm). The balcony is popular for smoking and intimate conversations away from the main party. At night, it's poorly lit, adding to the danger.

## The City

**City:** Barcelona
**Country:** Spain (Catalonia)
**Population:** 1.6 million
**Known for:** Gaudi architecture, beaches, nightlife, La Rambla, football, Gothic Quarter, festivals

### Geography
- **Climate:** Mediterranean - hot summers, mild winters
- **Terrain:** Coastal, built against hills
- **Weather during story:** Late September, La Merce festival - still warm (22-25C), occasional evening breeze from the sea

### Culture
- **Language(s):** Catalan and Spanish
- **Religion(s):** Historically Catholic, largely secular now
- **Customs relevant to story:** La Merce festival (September 24) - fire runs, human towers, concerts, heavy drinking. The Gothic Quarter becomes one massive party.

### Tourist Scene
- **Backpacker popularity:** Very High (one of Europe's top destinations)
- **Type of tourists:** Everything - party crowds, culture seekers, beach lovers, football fans
- **Danger level:** Generally safe, but pickpocketing and tourist-targeted crime common

## Hostel Atmosphere

### Day Vibe
Recovery mode - guests sleeping until noon, nursing hangovers in the courtyard. A few cultural tourists head out to see Gaudi. The rooftop is closed for cleaning and prep.

### Night Vibe
Transforms completely. Pre-drinks start at 8pm, rooftop bar opens at 9pm, peak party at midnight. During La Merce, the energy is electric - everyone is there for the same reason: to celebrate until sunrise.

### Guest Demographics
Mostly 20-30 year olds, heavy on Americans, Brits, and Australians. Gap year students, recent graduates, young professionals on holiday. A few older travelers who picked the wrong week for a quiet trip.

### Reputation
"Best rooftop bar in the Gothic Quarter." "Great for meeting people." "Staff is super chill." "Watch your stuff though." "Wild during La Merce - amazing."
